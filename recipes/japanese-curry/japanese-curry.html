Japanese Curry

Description

Japanese curry is a dish originating from Japan, which consists primarily of a curry sauce with vegetables, along with either beef, pork, or chicken, usualy fried in cutlets.

It is one of the most popular dishes in Japan and comes from the British who brought the idea of curry back from India to Japan.

Ingredients

  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 small potatoes
  • 300ml water
  • 6 blocks of Japanese curry
  • 500g boneless skinless chicken thigh

Steps

  1. Chop the vegetables into similarly sized cubes, around 2 cm sides, and add them to a large wok with a little bit of oil
  2. Stir fry for a few minutes, until some color forms, and then add the water.
  3. Let the vegetables cook for approximately 15 minutes, or until well tender and a fork pierces through.
  4. Meanwhile, in a separate pan, pan fry the chicken thighs until well cooked and browned.
  5. Finally, add the chicken in the work with the vegetables along with the curry cubes. It will take a minute for the cubes to melt.
  6. Serve over rice with a little bit of spring onion and sesame seeds. Enjoy!!!
