Nasi Goreng

Description

Nasi goreng is an Indonesian fried rice dish, consisting of rice, vegetables, meat, and a specific sauce called Kecap Manis, or simply sweet soy sauce.

It is traditionally served as breakfast, and is loved by many Southeast Asian countries, like Malaysia, Smingapore, and Brunei, who have all adapted the dish into their own versions.

Ingredients

  • 500g day old rice
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 7 cloves of garlic
  • 1 carrot
  • 3 king oyster mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 5 tbsp kecap manis
  • 500g shrimp, chopped

Steps

  1. Add the sesame oil in a large wok, and once heated through, add the minced garlic, and cook until fragrant.
  2. Meanwhile, chop all the vegetables into uniform sized cubes, around 5 mm sides, and then add them all to the wok with the garlic.
  3. Stir fry the vegetables until soft and fragrant, around 5 minutes.
  4. Next, remove the vegetables and set them in a plate aside, and add in the chopped shrimps.
  5. Stir fry for 1 minute, and then add the day old COLD rice to the wok, breaking the large chunks apart.
  6. Finally, add the kecap manis, and cook until the sauce caramelizes around the rice, around 2 minutes on high heat.
  7. Optionally, sprinkle some chopped spring onion and sesame seed, and serve with a sunny side up fried egg!
